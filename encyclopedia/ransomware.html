Ransomware

What is Ransomware?

Ransomware is a type of malware that encrypts the victim's files or locks their computer, demanding a ransom payment to restore access. It's one of the most profitable and dangerous malware types.

How It Works

  • Infects computer through email, vulnerabilities, or malicious downloads
  • Encrypts all accessible files using strong encryption
  • Displays ransom note with payment instructions
  • Demands payment in cryptocurrency
  • Threatens to delete files if ransom isn't paid

Real-World Example

WannaCry (2017): A global ransomware attack that affected hundreds of thousands of computers in over 150 countries, including the UK's National Health Service. It exploited a Windows vulnerability and demanded $300-$600 in Bitcoin.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Regularly back up your files to offline storage
  • Keep all software updated
  • Don't click suspicious links or attachments
  • Use reputable antivirus software
  • Never pay the ransom (no guarantee of recovery)
