Computer Virus

What is a Computer Virus?

A computer virus is a type of malicious software (malware) that can replicate itself and spread from one computer to another. It attaches itself to legitimate programs or files and activates when the host program runs.

How It Works

When a user runs an infected program or opens an infected file, the virus code executes and can:

  • Copy itself to other programs or files
  • Corrupt or delete data
  • Steal personal information
  • Damage system files
  • Spread to other computers via networks or removable media

Real-World Example

ILOVEYOU Virus (2000): One of the most destructive viruses ever, it spread via email with the subject "ILOVEYOU" and overwrote files on infected systems, causing an estimated $10 billion in damage worldwide.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Keep your antivirus software updated
  • Don't open email attachments from unknown senders
  • Be cautious when downloading files from the internet
  • Keep your operating system updated
  • Use strong passwords
  • regularly back up your important files
